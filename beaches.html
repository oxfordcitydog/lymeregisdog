Which Lyme Regis beaches allow dogs?

Lyme Regis is a genuinely good place to bring a dog, but the rules are not the same on every beach, and the one beach most visitors walk to first — the sandy one in the middle of town — is the one dogs cannot use in summer. Three of the four beaches welcome dogs all year round. Here is the plain version, written by someone who walks these beaches most mornings with a black Labrador.

Monmouth Beach

Dogs welcome all year round

The long pebble beach west of the Cobb, and the one we use most in summer. It stretches for about a mile, so even in August you can walk far enough along to find quiet space. The pebbles give way to rocky ledges as you go west — this is the famous Ammonite Pavement, where fossil spirals sit embedded in the rock. Beyond it the beach becomes the access point for the Undercliffs National Nature Reserve and the South West Coast Path towards Axmouth.

Worth knowing: pebbles get hot, and the walk in from the car park is longer than it looks. Leads are required on the slipways and sea walls getting there.

Church Cliff Beach

Dogs welcome all year round

East of the town, reached along Gunn Cliff Walk and the Church Cliff walkway. Quieter than Front Beach and popular with fossil hunters. Dogs must be on a lead near the sea wall above the beach itself.

Worth knowing: this is a tide-dependent beach. The sand goes quickly as the water comes in.

East Cliff Beach

Dogs welcome all year round

Further east again, continuing towards Charmouth, with big views back along the Jurassic Coast towards Golden Cap. Excellent for fossil hunting and rock pools, and usually the emptiest of the four. Reached via Church Cliff Walk, roughly ten minutes from Front Beach, with steps.

Worth knowing: the cliffs here are unstable — admire Black Ven from a distance and only pick up fossils already loose on the beach. Check the tide before you commit to walking east.

Front Beach (the main sandy beach)

Seasonal — no dogs 1 May to 30 September

The sandy beach on the promenade, beside the Cobb. This is the one with the seasonal ban: dogs are not permitted between 1 May and 30 September, and are welcome from 1 October to 30 April. Out of season it is a lovely winter dog beach — actual sand rather than pebbles, easy access, and cafés a few steps away.

Worth knowing: our Utilities page counts down the days until dogs are welcome back, so you don't have to remember the date.

🌊 A word about tides, because it matters here. The sand at Church Cliff and East Cliff vanishes fast on a rising tide, and people walking east towards Charmouth do get cut off — every year. Check the tide before you go, know when the water turns, and give yourself more time coming back than you think you need. Tide predictions on our Utilities page come from Open-Meteo and are estimates for planning a walk, not for navigation. Where dogs must be on a lead

Away from the beaches themselves, dogs need to be on a lead along Marine Parade, Cart Road, Bell Cliff, the harbour and the Cobb, the sea walls and slipways, Ozone Terrace, the entrance to Cobb Road, the public car parks and boat park, Cobb Gate car park, Gunn Cliff Walk, the Church Cliff walkway, and the steps up to Charmouth Road car park. In practice: lead on through the town and along the front, off on the dog-friendly beaches once you are down there and your dog is under control.

Getting there: parking and buses

Lyme Regis is steep, and where you park decides how much of a climb you and your dog face on the way back. Charmouth Road car park is the main long-stay option above the town, with steps down to the seafront. Holmbush is the other big one, up on the west side. Cobb Gate and Monmouth Beach car parks are closer to the water and fill early in summer. Current charges and locations are on the Dorset Council parking pages, and the town council lists local facilities at lymeregistowncouncil.gov.uk.

If you would rather not drive in at all, the X51 and X53 bus services connect Lyme Regis with Axminster, Bridport and Dorchester, and Axminster is the nearest railway station, about five miles away. Dogs travel free on most local buses at the driver's discretion. Timetables are on Traveline South West.

Common questions

Are dogs allowed on Lyme Regis beaches?

On three of them, all year: Monmouth, Church Cliff and East Cliff. Front Beach, the sandy one in the middle of town, is closed to dogs from 1 May to 30 September and open to them from 1 October to 30 April.

Which beach is best with a dog in summer?

Monmouth Beach. It is dog-friendly year round and long enough that you can walk away from the crowds. Early morning or evening is kindest on hot days — the pebbles hold heat.

Can my dog be off the lead?

Yes on the dog-friendly beaches, provided they are under control. No along the promenade, harbour, Cobb, sea walls and car parks, where leads are required.

Is there anywhere to get water for a dog?

Several cafés along the front will fill a bowl if you ask, and many put one outside in summer. Our dog-friendly cafés page has community reviews of who is genuinely welcoming rather than merely tolerant.

Can dogs go fossil hunting on the beach?

Yes — Monmouth, Church Cliff and East Cliff are all fossil beaches and all dog-friendly. Keep dogs away from the cliff bases, which are unstable, and watch paws on the sharper ledges.
