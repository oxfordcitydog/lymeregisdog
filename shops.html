Shopping in Lyme Regis with a dog

Lyme Regis is a town of small independent shops, and that is very good news if you have a dog with you. Independents make their own rules, and here those rules are usually generous: a great many shops in the town will happily have a dog inside, and a fair few keep a jar of biscuits behind the counter for regulars. The reliable exception is the supermarkets, which admit assistance dogs only.

The practical difficulties here are rarely about permission. They are about the shape of the town. Broad Street is steep and its pavements are narrow, several shops have a step or a tight doorway, and the artisan quarter's lanes were built long before anyone considered a Labrador with a wagging tail near a shelf of pottery. A short lead solves nearly all of it.

What to expect, by area

Broad Street and the town centre

Best for: browsing, bookshops, gifts, everyday errands

The main run of shops, climbing the hill from the seafront. Mostly independents, mostly welcoming, with a good mix of books, gifts, clothing and practical shops. The pavements are the narrowest in town and busy in summer, so keep leads short and expect to pause a lot. There is a steady stream of other dogs, which is either lovely or exhausting depending on your dog.

The artisan quarter and the lanes

Best for: galleries, studios, handmade things

The smaller lanes hold studios, galleries and craft shops. Many welcome dogs, but these are small spaces full of breakable and expensive things — worth a glance through the window first to judge whether there is room to manoeuvre, and worth asking at the door.

The seafront and Marine Parade

Best for: beach kit, ice cream, fossils, browsing between walks

Kiosks and shops along the front, with the advantage that most business happens at a hatch or doorway, so there is rarely any question about bringing a dog. Handy for buying what you forgot before hitting the beach. Remember dogs must be on a lead along Marine Parade and the harbour.

🐾 The bowl test. The most reliable sign in this town is a water bowl outside the door — a shop that puts one out has thought about dogs and will almost certainly let yours in. Failing that, ask. In four years of asking, being refused is rare enough to be memorable.

Buying for the dog

Dog food, treats, poo bags, leads, toys and beach towels are all available in the town without needing to drive to a retail park. Several general and hardware shops stock the basics, and the pet supplies you would expect are here too. If you have run out of something specific, the community reviews above are the fastest way to find who stocks what — and if you find something useful, please add it, because the "where do I buy dog food in Lyme Regis?" question comes up constantly with visitors.

The supermarket problem

The supermarkets admit assistance dogs only, which catches out a lot of visitors doing a holiday food shop. Options: take turns waiting outside, shop early before the town wakes up, or use the independents on Broad Street for the essentials instead, which is more expensive but considerably more pleasant. Tying a dog up outside a shop is common practice here but not something we would recommend for long, particularly in summer heat or with a nervous dog.

Common questions

Can I take my dog into shops in Lyme Regis?

Into most independent shops, yes — this is a notably dog-friendly town. Supermarkets are the exception, admitting assistance dogs only. A water bowl by the door is a good sign; otherwise just ask.

Are dogs allowed in the supermarkets?

No, assistance dogs only. Plan your food shop around that.

Where do I buy dog food if I have run out?

The town has pet supplies and several shops stocking food, treats and basics. Check the community reviews above for current information, since stock and shops change.

Is it manageable with a big or nervous dog?

Manageable, with planning. Broad Street's pavements are narrow and busy in season, and shop doorways are small. Early mornings and the shoulder seasons are far easier than August afternoons.

Is there anywhere to leave a dog while I shop?

There are no dog crèches in the town. Most people take turns, or shop at quiet times. If you do tie up outside, keep it brief, stay in sight, and never in direct summer sun — see the heat check on our Utilities page.
